Signed commits matter because they provide cryptographic proof that a commit truly came from a verified developer, not an impersonator who stole their credentials. This prevents attackers from injecting malicious code under a fake identity and ensures the integrity of project's history. Signed commits establish a verifiable chain of custody for all code changes, which is essential for meeting security compliance and audit requirements.
